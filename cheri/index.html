Use what enforces.
Build what means.

The thesis is substrate-is-regulation — so the regulation is the specification. The specification language and its compiler are what must be built; every substrate that can be coerced into obeying it should be rented, not rebuilt. Enforcers are silicon, solved logic, or shipped engines. Rebuild one and you forfeit its proofs and its ecosystem for a quarter of re-derivation. The moat is the semantic spine and the lowering — nothing beneath it.

03 — WHERE "USE" DOES NOT EXIST

Two builds forced by physics

Everywhere else, an enforcer can be rented. In exactly these two places the market and the physics leave a gap — there is no way to buy out.

⚡ FORCED BUILD · 01

Effect-force-time / TTL

Every enforcer in the stack is timeless or snapshot-stateless. CHERI carries no clock; Rego and Cedar evaluate a frozen instant. This is the one field the primitive carries that no substrate does.

Same seam as the render-gap: meaning that no substrate natively represents. It must live in the build layer or it does not exist.
⚡ FORCED BUILD · 02

The lowering

The compiler taking one annotated schema and emitting XDP2 and eBPF and CHERI otype-discipline and Cedar decisions is what makes "specification IS regulation" real.

Definitionally un-rentable — it is the encoding of the primitive. This is the artifact, and the moat.

DESIGN NOTE
⬡ THE TRAP OF THE CAPABLE SUBSTRATE ENGINEER

The pull is common among systems architects with the depth to build the substrate. The instinct — and identity — reaches for a bespoke capability engine, because the deep capability problem is the beautiful one and the moat feels like it lives in silicon. It does not.

The moat is the compiler and the primitive; the substrate is a commodity to be coerced. Every hour on a bespoke capability engine or a hand-rolled policy DSL is an hour stolen from the lowering that is the thesis. The discipline is refusal: decline the substrate build precisely because it is the one build within reach.

Build the meaning. Rent the enforcement. Ship the barrier.
LATENT NOTIONS · TLDR

Latent notions — questions · risks · edges

  • BORROW is the fragile category. "Borrow Cerise's methodology" silently becomes a quarter of Iris/Coq. Tripwire: touch Coq only if a customer or regulator demands a machine-checked barrier proof.
  • otype width vs dialect type-count. A rented enforcer imposes a hard ceiling on a built layer. Virtualization is a build that has not yet been scoped.
  • Cedar can't hold TTL. If temporal logic must live in the build layer regardless, Cedar's marginal value over the native Rego path narrows. Re-weigh deliberately.
  • Forced-build sequencing. The lowering and the temporal enforcer are coupled — force-time is a field the compiler must lower. Neither ships without a decision on the other.

TLDR

USE = enforcement — CHERI, VeriCHERI, Sail, LLVM, MLIR-infra, Rego, Cedar engine.

BORROW = theorems — Cerise, TAL. Reading, not adoption.

BUILD = meaning — the attachment primitive, temporal enforcer, unified dialect, the lowering, thin Cedar/provenance glue.

FORCED by physics: TTL/force-time and the lowering — nobody rents those. The temptation is to build the substrate; the discipline is to refuse it and build the compiler.
